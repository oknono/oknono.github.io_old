With some delay I set out to visit Angkor Wat. Since I spend all my money on a tour before, I decided to just wing it, and cycle around and look at the ruins without the baggage of knowledge. Overrated! Angkor Wat is huge and impressive. That’s about all I can tell since I didn’t have a tour guide and didn’t do my research. Pro tip: You can eavesdrop on other tour groups that are not stingy and did get an tour guide. Good way to practice your French as well!

I thought the pictogram bottom left meant “forbidden to meditate” and that made me giggle for too long, as I could see the destruction caused by thousands of meditating white people in elephant pants. The true meaning is half as exciting.

Again, some serious Legend of Zelda Forest Temple Action

Yup, it’s another temple

And a statue. If you want to know why all the statues are headless, Google it. (and with Google I mean Duck Duck Go)

It made me want to see the Alhambra again. It made me want to see Europe again. What am I doing in Asia, when there is still so much to explore. Paris! Madrid! Porto! London! Florence! I made me want to have children so I could drag them along with me and show them the world, the good and the bad, the beautiful and the ugly! And then BABY MONKIES showed up and I forgot everything I was thinking before. How does one get a baby monkey! How did MJ get away with Bubbles?! Where is Bubbles!??! BABY MONKIES!!!!!

OMG LOOK AT THE BABY MONKEY!

On my way back to the hostel I took a detour and had a superb Vietnamese sandwich and saw a lot of Siem Reap. This is not because I am such an adventurous person, but more because the traffic is scary and left turns seem suicidal so I just kept going straight.

My only true dream is to be the female Anthony Bourdain
